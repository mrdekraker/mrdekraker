# Mark DeKraker — Welcome Home

*A Catholic theology blog built for the kitchen table.*

---

## About This Site

**Mark DeKraker** is a husband, father, musician, and Catholic convert — confirmed Easter 2026, Filipino-born, Canadian by citizenship, and rooted in Texas. This site is where he works through the intellectual and spiritual tradition of the Church one question at a time, in public.

The tagline is simple: **Welcome home.** It's an arrival, an invitation, and a theological statement all at once. Whether you're a fellow convert, a cradle Catholic trying to pass the faith on to your kids, or someone curious about where the Catholic road goes — you're welcome here.

---

## What You'll Find Here

Writing across a few threads:

- **Faith & Reason** — The intellectual tradition of the Church. Natural law, the arguments, the great thinkers. Newman, Aquinas, Chesterton. Theology that welcomes the questions rather than suspecting them.
- **Family Formation** — What it actually looks like to pass the faith on to young children. Not theory — the kitchen table version.
- **The Convert's Journey** — Written from inside a tradition still being learned. Protestant for over two decades before coming home to Rome at Easter 2026.
- **The Examined Life** — Tolkien, Homer, the classics. The conviction that beauty is an argument and that ordinary things carry more meaning than they appear to.

## The Person Behind It

Born in the Philippines. Raised in Texas. Canadian by citizenship, working toward naturalization. Adopted into a family with Chinese, German, and Dutch roots — which is to say, the long way home is not a metaphor. It's just his life.

Patron saint: **St. Mark the Evangelist** — author of the most urgent Gospel, the one who didn't linger. A writer who understood the good news was too important to get in the way of.

Influences: Newman, Chesterton, Aquinas, Tolkien, Homer, and twenty-some years of Protestant formation that didn't disappear when he crossed the Tiber — it came with him.
